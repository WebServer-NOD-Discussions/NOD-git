Get to know and know how to...

This section introduces the user to the different Modes of operation for NOD. The following information is intended to get the user acquainted with the NOD drug repurposing pipeline and make a decision on the best use-case operation for their scientific query.

Mode-1

The MODE-1 of the pipeline should be used when the user is interested in finding potential repurpose-able candidates against multiple query protein sequences of choice. For example: When a user is interested in getting a list of potential drugs/drug candidates which can be repurposed against:

  1. all the proteins encoded in the genome of a pathogenic organism like P. falciparum, M. tuberculosis, SARS-CoV-2 etc.
  2. a set of proteins involved in a specific biological pathway that play a role in a disease condition like proteins involved in bacterial cell wall synthesis, proteins involved cancer signalling pathways etc.
  3. multiple orthologous proteins of interest from different organisms, such as main proteases of different species of coronaviruses.
  4. any set protein sequences of userâ€™s interest.

Results

Upon completion, an output table containing details of potential DrugBank molecules which could be targeted against the protein(s) of interest are listed.

Mode-2

MODE-2 of the pipeline should be used when the user is interested in finding potential repurpose-able candidates against only one particular protein, for example, dihydrofolate reductase of Mtb, main protease of SARS-CoV-2, human aurora kinase-A/B/C etc. This mode of the pipeline is faster than MODE-1 (since here only one protein sequence is queried vs. multiple protein sequences in MODE-1) and also comes with an advantage of an extra analysis. After the initial list of repurpose-able candidates (based on the homologous associations identified between the queried protein sequence and the DrugBank dataset of protein sequences) are fetched, chemically similar molecules from the approved subset of small molecules in DrugBank are also listed. This helps in enhancing the chemical space of the potential repurpose-able agents against the queried target protein.

Results

Upon completion, an output table containing primary candidates (based on homology search) are listed. The table is further extended with approved molecules that are structurally (2D) similar to the primary candidate.

We advise user discretion while selecting candidates from the results table. There might be metal ions or nutraceutical which shall be removed before processing the final results.....
